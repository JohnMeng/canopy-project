1.1 Getting Started: About Cloud-Enabled Devices

Technological Trends That Are Enabling IoT

There is certainly a lot of buzz about the "Internet of Things" these days. But looking past the hype, there are some powerful technological trends at play that are changing the way we interact with physical devices.

Cheap & Small Processors

First of all, demand from the smartphone industry has driven down the cost, size, and power consumption of microprocessors. On the one hand, this can be simply be viewed as Moore's Law at play. On the other hand, Moore's Law is just a statement about transistors density increasing over time. It was the smartphone industry that got everyone to shift focus from building more and more powerful computers, to cheaper and less power-hungry computers.

The Cloud

The second trend enabling IoT is the maturity and commoditization of cloud infrastructure. It is easier than ever to build scalable, highly-available cloud-based services, using tools like AWS and NoSQL databases. For the past several years, this trend has been driven by demand for web services and cloud-based apps.

Improved Wireless

Lastly, wireless technology is undergoing a silent revolution. New technologies like Bluetooth LE, 6LOPAN, are Thread and simplifying RF communications and bringing down the cost and power consumption of wireless interactions.

The Result is IoT

These trends together are fundamentally changing how consumers interact with products.

With cheap, small & efficient processors, we can start to put a CPU in everything.

With cheap, small & efficient wireless, we can start to connect everything to the Internet.

With cheap and scalable cloud infrastructure, we can start to monitor and control everything over the Internet (through smartphone apps).
